    or lemon juice.

  2. They turn blue litmus red.

Arrhenius concept of Acids and bases

According to the concept(1787):

Acid is a substance which dissociate in aqueous solution to give hydrodgen
ions.

      In genertal the ionization of acids,take place as follows.

HA →waterH++A-

For example,substance such as HCl,HNO3,CH3COOH,HCN,etc.,are acids
because they ionize in aqueous to provide H+ ions.

       HCl →water  H++Cl-

      HNO3→waterH++ NO3-

Base is a substance which dissociate in water to give hydroxide ions

     The general formula of Base take place as follows:

       BOH →waterB++OH-

The substances such as NaOH,KOH,NH4.,etc,are bases becacuse
these compounds ionize in aqueous solutions ti provide OH- ions.

      NaOH→waterNa++OH-

      CaOH→water Ca+2+2(OH)-

Thus, according to Concept:

Acids give H+ions in water and bases give OH-ions in water.

limitations of Arrhenius concept:-

  1. This concept is applicable only in aqueous medium and does not explain
    nature of acid and base in non-aqueous medium.

  2. According to the concept,acids and bases are only those compounds which
    contain Hydrogen and Hydroxide ions,respectively.It can't explain the nature
    of componds like CO2,NH3.,etc,which are acid and base

Brownsted-Lowry Concept

In 1923,the Danish chemist Bronsted and the German chemist Lowry independently
presented their theories of acid and base on the basis of proton transfer.

Acid is a substance that donate a proton from another substance.

Base is a substance that accapt a proton from another substance.

For example ,HCl acts as an acid while NH3acts as a base.

      HCl + NH3→ NH4+ + Cl-

Conjugate Acid:-

A conjugate acid is a specie fpormed by accepting a proton by base.

Conjugate Base:-

A conjugate base is a specie formed by donating a proton by acid.

Thus,their example as follows:-

HCL+ H2O →H3O      +    Cl-
  Acid  base      conjugate-   conjugate-
                         acid             base

According to the Brownsted-Lowry concept,An acid and a base always work
to transfer a proton.THat means ,a substance can act an acid only when
another substance simultaneouslybehaves a base.HENce ,a substance can
act as an acid as well as base ,depending upon the nature of another substance.

Amphoteric:-

A substance that behave an acid asd well as base is an Amphoteric.

Limitations:-

It has been observed that their are certain substance that behaves an acid
though they do not have the ability to donate a proton .,e.g,SO3.

Similarly,CaO behaves as a base but it has not abilityto accept a proton.

Lewis Concept of acid and base

The Arrhenius and Brownsted-Lowry concept are limited to substancer that
contain protons.G.N.Lewis proposed a more general and broader concept of acid
and base

Acidis a substance which can accept a pair of electrons.

Baseis a substance which can donate a pair of electrons.

For example,a reaction between Ammonia and Boron Trifluoride take place by forming
a coordinate covalent bond between ammonia and boron trifluoride by donating an
elctron pair of ammonia and boron accepting a pair of electron by boron trifluoride.

Lewis Acid;

    Na+ions etc.,have a very little tendency to accept an
    electrons.While the cations like H+ions,etc.,have a greater

According to the Lewis concept, the following specie can act as

lewis base

Indicators

Indicators are the organic compounds.They have different colours in acidic and
basic solutions.Litmus is a common indicator.It is red in acidic solution
and blue in alkaline soiution.

Each indicator has a specific colour in acidic medium which changes at a specific pH to another colour in basic medium.

Salts

Salts are ionic compounds generally formed by the nurtralization of an acid and base.

Salts are made up of positive and negative ions .A cation ia metallic ion is derived
from a base ,therefore,it is called basic radical.While anion is derived from acid therefore it is called acid radical

A salt gets its name from the name of the metal and the acid.

Characteristics of Salts:-

  1. Salts are the organic compounds found in crystalline solid.

  2. They have high melting and boiling points.

  3. Most of the salts contains water of crystallization which is responsible for the shape of the crystals.
    Number of molecules of water are specific for each salt and they are written with the chemical formula of salt.
    For example,
    Copper sulphate=CuSO4.5H2O

  4. Salts are nuetral compounds.Although,they do not compose of equal number
    of positive and negative ions,but they have equal numbers of positive and negative
